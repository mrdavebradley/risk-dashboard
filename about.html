Understanding the CRSS

The Composite Risk Sentiment Score (CRSS) is a rule-based quantitative model designed to measure the "pulse" of global financial markets.

The Philosophy

Most investors make the mistake of looking at the S&P 500 in isolation. However, price action often masks underlying fragility. The CRSS was built to solve the "Noise Problem" by aggregating 11 distinct data points across equity, credit, currency, and commodity markets into a single, actionable signal.

By using a rules-based approach, we strip away the emotional bias of news cycles and focus on where the money is actually moving.

The Multi-Asset Dataset

Volatility & Sentiment

  • VIX Index: The "Fear Gauge" measuring expected equity volatility.
  • Bitcoin (BTC): A high-beta liquidity proxy that thrives in high risk-appetite regimes.
  • Money Market Inflows: Measures capital flight into cash "sidelines."

Credit & Liquidity

  • High-Yield Spreads: Measures corporate credit stress and default risk.
  • 10Y Treasury Yield: The benchmark "Risk-Free" rate that dictates global valuations.
  • DXY (US Dollar): The global liquidity drain; a rising dollar is usually a headwind for risk.

Participation & Breadth

  • RSP/SPY Ratio: Measures if the "Average" stock is keeping up with the Tech Giants.
  • IWM (Small Caps): Gauges domestic growth and "junk" equity performance.
  • SPY Momentum: The primary trend-following component of the S&P 500.

Commodities

  • Copper: "Dr. Copper" serves as a proxy for global industrial expansion.
  • Gold: The classic defensive anchor and inflation hedge.

The Mathematical Framework

1. Normalization (The Z-Score)

Markets are built of different units (Dollars, Percentages, Volatility Points). To compare them, we calculate a rolling 1-year Z-Score for every asset. This measures how far the current price is from its 52-week average. This prevents a volatile asset like Bitcoin from drowning out a stable one like Treasury Yields.

2. Strategic Weighting

Not all indicators are created equal. We assign weights based on an indicator's historical reliability as a "Lead Signal":

High-Yield Spreads (15%) VIX Index (12%) DXY (10%) Small Caps (10%) Copper (5%)

Interpreting the Regimes

RISK ON (+35 to +100)

High conviction. Capital is flowing aggressively into growth assets. Bullish sentiment dominates.

NEUTRAL (-35 to +35)

Indecision. The market is in a "wait and see" mode, likely consolidating before a major move.

RISK OFF (-100 to -35)

Flight to safety. Investors are selling growth and buying defensives (Cash, Gold, Treasuries).
